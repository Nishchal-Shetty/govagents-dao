Bug Bounty Program: 90-Day Security Incentive Initiative

## Summary

Establish a time-bounded bug bounty program to incentivise external security researchers to responsibly disclose vulnerabilities in the DAO's deployed contracts.

## Motivation

The core contracts (DAOGovernance.sol and associated treasury logic) have been audited internally but have not been exposed to independent external review under economic incentive. A bug bounty lowers the cost of finding vulnerabilities before they are exploited and signals to the broader community that the DAO takes security seriously.

## Specification

Duration: 90 days from proposal execution date.

Scope: DAOGovernance.sol, GovernanceToken.sol, TreasuryVault.sol (deployed addresses on record). Out of scope: front-end, off-chain infrastructure, third-party integrations.

Reward tiers:
  - Critical (fund loss, access control bypass): up to 20,000 USDC
  - High (significant fund risk, reliable DoS): up to 10,000 USDC
  - Medium (limited exploit requiring preconditions): up to 3,000 USDC
  - Low / Informational: up to 500 USDC

Total budget cap: 50,000 USDC drawn from the security reserve sub-account, not from the main treasury allocation.

Responsible disclosure process: researchers submit findings via a designated encrypted email address. The core team has 7 days to acknowledge and 30 days to remediate before public disclosure is permitted. Disputes escalated to a 3-member security committee.

No new on-chain permissions are required. No contract changes are made by this proposal. Payouts are executed manually by the multisig upon verified submission.

## Success Metrics

Number of valid reports filed, severity distribution, and time-to-remediation logged publicly at the end of the 90-day window.
